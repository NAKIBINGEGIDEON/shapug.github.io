About Us

KAN Sustainable Health Action for Development (SHAP-UGANDA) Limited is an indigenous Not-For-Profit Non-Governmental Organization established in 2021. It was founded by a group of passionate public health professionals with the aim of influencing development and providing sustainable, integrated, and quality comprehensive healthcare services to the most marginalized and vulnerable individuals in the community.

We operate in the Districts of Kamuli, Luuka, Buyende, Kayunga, and Namutumba, implementing our interventions in the technical program areas of:

  • Integrated Reproductive Maternal, Newborn, Child, and Adolescent Health services
  • Prevention and control of major communicable diseases
  • Health promotion and community health initiatives
  • Environmental health and school health
  • Health system strengthening and gender equality

Our target beneficiaries include Adolescent girls, boys and young women (AGYW), Orphans and Vulnerable Children (OVC), Women, Youths, Key and Priority Populations, People living with HIV (PLHIV), and People living with Disabilities (PLWD).

Vision

A transformed community for better health outcomes.

Mission

To improve accessibility to sustainable quality healthcare through empowering vulnerable communities to embrace healthcare services and strengthening existing structures at different levels.

Objectives / Aims

Strategic Objectives / Program Areas

  • Community health system strengthening and health promotion
  • Community economic empowerment

Specific Objectives

  • Promote the availability, accessibility, and utilization of healthcare services through awareness creation and resource mobilization.
  • Support the Government of Uganda in the implementation of Minimum Primary Health Care Packages.
  • Contribute to a socially, economically, legally, and politically empowered community through advocacy and capacity building.
  • Collaborate with existing stakeholders toward providing quality healthcare services.
  • Contribute towards child protection services in the community.
