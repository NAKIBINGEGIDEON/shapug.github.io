ABOUT US

KAN Sustainable Health Action for Development (SHAP-UGANDA) Limited

KAN Sustainable Health Action for Development (SHAP-UGANDA) Limited is an indigenous Not-For-Profit Non-Governmental Organization that started in 2021 by a group of passionate public health professionals. The organization aims to influence development, provide sustainable, integrated, and quality comprehensive health care services to marginalized and vulnerable individuals in the community through capacity building, empowerment, community-driven health interventions, and community participation.

We operate in the Districts of Kamuli, Luuka, Buyende, Kayunga, and Namutumba implementing our interventions in the technical program areas of Integrated Reproductive Maternal, Newborn, Child, and Adolescent Health services, prevention and control of major communicable diseases and non-communicable diseases, health promotion, community health initiatives, environmental health, school health, health system strengthening, as well as gender and health.

Our target beneficiaries include Adolescent Girls, Boys and Young Women (AGYW), Orphans and Vulnerable Children (OVC), Women, Youth, Key and Priority Populations, People Living with HIV (PLHIV), and People Living with Disabilities (PLWD).

VISION, MISSION, VALUES & PRINCIPLES

Vision

A transformed community for better health outcomes.

Mission

To improve accessibility to sustainable quality health care through empowering vulnerable communities to embrace health care services and strengthening existing structures at different levels.

OBJECTIVES

Strategic Objectives/Program Areas
  • Community health system strengthening and health promotion: Capacity building of health service providers, health activities implementation, health system and structural development, health research, and ONE health programming.
  • Community economic empowerment: Crosscutting program areas like Gender, inclusion, child protection, and climate change adaptation.
Specific Objectives
  • Promote the availability, accessibility, and utilization of health care services through awareness and resource mobilization.
  • Support the Government of Uganda in the implementation of Minimum Primary Health Care Packages.
  • Contribute to a socially, economically, legally, and politically empowered community through advocacy and capacity building.
  • Collaborate with existing stakeholders for the provision of quality health care services.
  • Contribute towards child protection services in the community.
