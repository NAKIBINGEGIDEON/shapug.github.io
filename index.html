About Us

Our Mission

KAN Sustainable Health Action for Development (SHAP-UGANDA) Limited is a Not-For-Profit Non-Governmental Organization, founded in 2021 by a group of public health professionals with the goal of providing comprehensive healthcare services to marginalized communities in Uganda.

Our Work

We focus on delivering quality health services through community-driven interventions in the districts of Kamuli, Luuka, Buyende, Kayunga, and Namutumba. We work in areas such as:

  • Maternal, Newborn, and Child Health
  • Prevention and control of communicable and non-communicable diseases
  • Health promotion and community health initiatives
  • Environmental and school health
  • Gender and health initiatives

Our Target Groups

Our target beneficiaries include:

  • Adolescent Girls, Boys, and Young Women (AGYW)
  • Orphans and Vulnerable Children (OVC)
  • Women, Youth, and Priority Populations
  • People Living with HIV (PLHIV) and People Living with Disabilities (PLWD)

Our Approach

We work with local communities, empowering them to access healthcare services and strengthening existing structures for long-term sustainability. Our approach emphasizes capacity building, community participation, and inclusion.

Vision

A transformed community for better health outcomes.

Mission

To improve accessibility to sustainable quality healthcare through empowering vulnerable communities to embrace healthcare services and strengthening existing structures at different levels.

Objectives / Aims

Strategic Objectives / Program Areas

  • Community health system strengthening and health promotion
  • Community economic empowerment

Specific Objectives

  • Promote the availability, accessibility, and utilization of healthcare services through awareness creation and resource mobilization.
  • Support the Government of Uganda in the implementation of Minimum Primary Health Care Packages.
  • Contribute to a socially, economically, legally, and politically empowered community through advocacy and capacity building.
  • Collaborate with existing stakeholders toward providing quality healthcare services.
  • Contribute towards child protection services in the community.
