About SHAP-Uganda

KAN Sustainable Health Action for Development (SHAP-Uganda) Limited is an indigenous, Not-For-Profit Non-Governmental Organization established in 2021. It was founded by a group of passionate public health professionals to influence development and provide sustainable, integrated, and quality health care services to marginalized and vulnerable communities.

We operate in the Districts of Kamuli, Luuka, Buyende, Kayunga, and Namutumba, implementing interventions in the technical areas of Integrated Reproductive Maternal, Newborn, Child, and Adolescent Health services, the prevention and control of major communicable diseases, health promotion, community health initiatives, environmental health, school health, and health system strengthening.

Our target beneficiaries include Adolescent Girls, Boys, Young Women (AGYW), Orphans and Vulnerable Children (OVC), Women, Youths, Key and Priority Populations, People Living with HIV (PLHIV), and People Living with Disabilities (PLWD).

Vision & Mission

Vision

A transformed community for better health outcomes.

Mission

To improve accessibility to sustainable quality health care by empowering vulnerable communities to embrace health care services and strengthening existing structures at different levels.

Principles

  • Prevention: Focusing on population and individual-based interventions to prevent diseases and ensure early detection for timely management.
  • Efficiency: Implementing interventions with optimal utilization of resources for maximum health results.
  • Primacy of Localism: Actively involving the community and engaging local leaders in the decision-making process.

Core Values

  • Transparency: Open communication about resources, funding, and project progress.
  • Inclusiveness & Equity: Non-discriminatory services based on gender, age, ethnicity, socio-economic status, and disability.
  • Professionalism & Probity: Upholding ethics, respect, and timely communication in the workplace.
  • Innovativeness & Sustainability: Encouraging creativity while ensuring long-term sustainability through community participation.

Objectives & Aims

Strategic Objectives

  • Community health system strengthening and health promotion through capacity building and health activities.
  • Community economic empowerment through gender inclusion, child protection, and climate change adaptation.

Specific Objectives

  • Promote the availability and accessibility of health care services in communities through awareness and resource mobilization.
  • Support the Government of Uganda in implementing Minimum Primary Health Care Packages.
  • Advocate for and contribute towards a socially, economically, legally, and politically empowered community.
  • Collaborate with stakeholders to provide quality health care services.
  • Contribute to child protection services in the community.
