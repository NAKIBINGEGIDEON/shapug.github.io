About SHAP-Uganda

1.1 Name of the Organization

KAN Sustainable Health Action for Development (SHAP-UGANDA) Limited is an indigenous Not-For-Profit Non-Governmental Organization that was founded in 2021 by a group of passionate public health professionals.

1.2 About Us

SHAP-Uganda focuses on influencing development by providing sustainable, integrated, and quality comprehensive healthcare services to marginalized and vulnerable individuals in the community. This is achieved through capacity building, empowerment, community-driven health interventions, and active participation.

We operate in the districts of Kamuli, Luuka, Buyende, Kayunga, and Namutumba, implementing interventions in areas such as Integrated Reproductive Maternal, Newborn, Child and Adolescent Health services, disease prevention, health promotion, community health initiatives, environmental health, school health, health system strengthening, and gender and health.

Our target beneficiaries include adolescent girls, boys and young women (AGYW), orphans and vulnerable children (OVC), women, youth, key populations, people living with HIV (PLHIV), and people living with disabilities (PLWD).

1.3 Registered Office

The registered office is located in Buyende Town Council, Buyende District, Uganda. The governing council may decide to change the address in the future.

2.0 Vision, Mission Statement, Values & Principles

2.1 Vision

A transformed community for better health outcomes.

2.2 Mission

To improve accessibility to sustainable quality health care through empowering vulnerable communities to embrace healthcare services and strengthening existing structures at different levels.

2.3 Principles

  • Prevention: Focus on population and individual-based interventions aimed at early disease detection and better health outcomes.
  • Efficiency: Implement interventions with optimal use of available resources for maximum health results.
  • Primacy of localism: Engage communities through active participation, involving community gatekeepers for effective operations.

2.4 Core Values

  • Transparency: Openly share our mission and vision with the public, and ensure accountability in the use of resources.
  • Inclusiveness & Equity: Ensure no discrimination based on gender, age, race, or disability, providing resources based on individual needs.
  • Professionalism & Probity: Uphold ethics, respect, communication, and dedication to service among staff and stakeholders.
  • Innovativeness & Sustainability: Encourage creativity, active participation, and sustainability in all activities.

3.0 Objectives / Aims

Strategic Objectives / Program Areas

  • Community health system strengthening and health promotion
  • Community economic empowerment

Specific Objectives

  • Promote the availability, accessibility, and utilization of healthcare services through awareness and resource mobilization.
  • Support the Government of Uganda in implementing Minimum Primary Health Care Packages.
  • Contribute to the empowerment of communities through advocacy and capacity building.
  • Collaborate with existing stakeholders to provide quality healthcare services.
  • Contribute to child protection services within the community.
